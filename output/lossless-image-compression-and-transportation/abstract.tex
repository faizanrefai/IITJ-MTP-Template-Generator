\huge{\textbf{Abstract}}

\vspace{0.5cm}



This project? It’s a deep dive into lossless image compression. Not just one or two methods. A whole bunch. We’re talking classical entropy stuff – Huffman, Arithmetic, LZW. But also spatial tricks, transform domain wizardry, context‑aware algorithms, clustering, optimization hacks, and even neural nets.

Let me back up.

First, you need the basics. So I implemented Huffman. Arithmetic coding too. And LZW. That gives you a feel for how entropy‑based methods work. They’re simple. They work okay. But they ignore the fact that pixels near each other tend to look alike.

So then I moved to spatial methods. Variable block size compression. Run‑length encoding – RLE for short. The idea? Find local repetition and squash it. Then there’s this clever trick: lossy plus residual. You send a lossy version first – smaller, uglier – then transmit the leftover difference. Reconstruct the original perfectly. Neat, right?

After that, I tackled the JPEG lossless prediction standard. Seven prediction modes. All based on neighbors – left, above, that sort of thing.

But the real heavy hitters came next. CALIC. LOCO‑I. These are context‑based beasts. They look at gradients. They model prediction errors in different contexts. And they crush it on compression ratio.

Transform domain? Yeah, did that too. S‑Transform. S+P Transform. Integer wavelet transforms using the lifting scheme. Why? Because these methods pack energy into fewer coefficients. Less numbers to store.

Clustering? You bet. K‑Means. Gath‑Geva fuzzy clustering. The latter is weird – it finds ellipsoidal clusters of different sizes and densities. Not your everyday circle stuff. Then I threw Particle Swarm Optimization at the problem. Swarm intelligence. Little virtual particles buzzing around, hunting for the best color palette.

And the cherry on top: a fuzzy backpropagation neural network. FBPNN. I combined Gath‑Geva’s fuzzy membership values with classic backprop learning. The network adapts its weights on the fly.

All this runs in Python. NumPy, Matplotlib, Pillow – the usual suspects. But I also used GPU acceleration – JAX and PyTorch MPS on an Apple M4 chip. Because why wait?

I generated lots of pictures. Original vs compressed. Compression ratios. MSE. PSNR. The whole nine yards.

What did I learn? Context‑based methods win. CALIC and LOCO‑I consistently beat the older approaches. No contest.
